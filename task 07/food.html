Traditional Food of Bihar

Bihar's cuisine is a delightful blend of flavors, ingredients, and cooking techniques that reflect the state's rich cultural heritage. Here are some traditional dishes that are integral to Bihari cuisine:

Litti Chokha

Litti:

Roasted dough balls stuffed with a mixture of spiced gram flour (sattu), herbs, and spices.

Chokha:

A mashed vegetable dish made from roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and potatoes, seasoned with mustard oil and spices.

Sattu Paratha:

Flatbread stuffed with roasted gram flour (sattu) mixed with spices, onions, and green chilies.

Thekua:

A traditional sweet made from wheat flour, jaggery, and ghee. It is commonly prepared during festivals like Chhath Puja. Dal Pitha: Rice flour dumplings stuffed with spiced lentils, steamed and served with chutney. Kadhi Badi: Gram flour dumplings cooked in a spiced yogurt sauce, usually served with rice. Tilkut: A sweet made from sesame seeds and jaggery, often enjoyed during the Makar Sankranti festival. Malpua: A sweet pancake made from a batter of flour, milk, and sugar, deep-fried and soaked in sugar syrup. Chana Ghugni: A spicy and tangy chickpea curry, often served as a snack or side dish. Khaja: A layered, crispy sweet made from wheat flour, sugar, and ghee, famous in regions like Silao and Rajgir. Balu Shahi: A flaky, deep-fried sweet soaked in sugar syrup, similar to a glazed doughnut. Bihari cuisine is known for its simplicity, use of locally sourced ingredients, and bold flavors. These traditional dishes are not only delicious but also carry the essence of Bihar's culinary heritage.
